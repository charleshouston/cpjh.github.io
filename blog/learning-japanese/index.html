Learning Japanese
cpjh
17 Aug, 2016
1 minute read

Over the past half year or so, I’ve been slowly building up my working knowledge of Japanese. After a formal education in French (and minor dabblings in German and Swedish), getting to grips with Japanese has been refreshing and exciting. Studying a humanities subject also provides a balancing contrast to my computational and mathematical field of research. My (very) long-term goal is to be able to read, listen and converse in the language.

Of the resources I’ve looked through, a few have been particularly useful. After learning Hiragana and Katakana, I tried the RTK method to learn some initial Kanji. However, I’ve recently started using WaniKani from the beginning and think its an excellent resource. For grammar, Tae Kim’s online guide is really excellent. There’s so much information here, presented in an easy-to-understand fashion.

The main purpose of this post is to put the notes I’ve started making for Tae Kim’s guide online. I’m directly using examples and text from the guide and aim to produce a much-condensed version for referral rather than learning. I’ve linked it here.
